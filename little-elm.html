Why People Love Living in Little Elm

Little Elm sits right on the shores of Lewisville Lake, and that changes everything about daily life here. You have beach access at the Little Elm Park, miles of hiking and biking trails, and water recreation year-round. The town has grown fast over the past decade, but it still has that small-town feel where neighbors know each other and the community shows up for events at the lakefront.

The housing market here is one of the best values in Denton County. The median home price is around $385K, which gets you a lot more house than you would find in nearby Frisco or Prosper. Master-planned communities like Paloma Creek and Eldorado offer newer construction with pools, playgrounds, and walking trails built right in. Young families are drawn to the strong schools in Little Elm ISD and parts of Denton ISD, plus the quick access to Frisco shopping and dining along the 380 corridor.

Little Elm Communities Worth Knowing

Little Elm has grown around master-planned communities and lakefront neighborhoods. Each one has its own character, amenities, and price range. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Paloma Creek

Paloma Creek is the largest master-planned community in Little Elm, and it is easy to see why it is so popular. You get a mix of single-family homes with community pools, playgrounds, trails, and a rec center. Homes range from the mid $300Ks to the low $500Ks depending on size and lot. It is a great fit for families who want newer construction, good schools, and a neighborhood where kids are outside playing every evening.

Eldorado

Eldorado is another well-established community in Little Elm with a resort-style amenity center, multiple pools, and walking trails. Homes here tend to be well-maintained and range from the $350Ks to the mid $400Ks. The community has a strong HOA that keeps common areas looking sharp. Buyers who want a turnkey neighborhood with plenty of family activities love Eldorado.

Sunset Pointe

Sunset Pointe sits closer to Lewisville Lake and offers some of the best lake proximity in Little Elm. Homes here tend to be slightly more premium, with larger lots and easy access to the water. If you want that lakefront lifestyle without the full lakefront price tag, Sunset Pointe is worth a serious look. Sellers in this community benefit from strong buyer demand year-round.

Lake Crest

Lake Crest is a newer development that has been attracting buyers who want modern floor plans and energy-efficient builds. The community is still growing, which means there are opportunities to get in at a good price before everything is built out. Prices start in the mid $300Ks for smaller homes and go up from there. It is a solid choice for first-time buyers or anyone looking for newer construction in Little Elm.
