Garland Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Garland has a lot of variety packed into one city. From lakefront living to established family neighborhoods, here's what our agents see on the ground.

Firewheel Area

North Garland's Firewheel area is one of the most sought-after parts of the city. Newer construction, well-maintained subdivisions, and direct access to Firewheel Town Center make this a top pick for families and professionals. Homes here tend to be priced above the Garland median, but you still get more for your money compared to neighboring Plano or Richardson. The schools in this part of Garland ISD are strong, and the shopping and dining are walkable.

Duck Creek

Duck Creek is a well-established neighborhood in central Garland with mature trees, larger lots, and solid mid-century homes. It's the kind of neighborhood where people know their neighbors and kids ride bikes on the sidewalks. The Duck Creek Trail runs through the area, giving residents a great greenway for walking, jogging, and biking. Homes here offer strong value, and it's an easy commute to downtown Dallas via I-30.

Eastern Hills

Eastern Hills is one of Garland's most affordable neighborhoods, making it a great entry point for first-time buyers. The homes are mostly ranch-style from the 1960s and 70s, with good bones and room for updates. Buyers who are willing to put in a little work can find real value here. The neighborhood is close to shopping, parks, and major highways. For investors, the rental demand in this area is solid.

Lake Ray Hubbard Area

The eastern edge of Garland borders Lake Ray Hubbard, and homes in this area come with a lifestyle upgrade. Waterfront and water-view properties, easy access to boat ramps and marinas, and the kind of sunsets that make you forget you're in the suburbs. Prices vary a lot depending on how close you are to the water, but even homes a few blocks from the lake benefit from the parks, trails, and recreation that come with lakefront living.

Common Questions About Garland Real Estate

How much is my Garland home worth right now?

The citywide median in Garland is around $310K, but prices vary quite a bit by neighborhood. Homes near Firewheel and Lake Ray Hubbard tend to be higher, while areas like Eastern Hills offer more affordable options. Fill out the form above and we'll send you a free report based on recent sales near your exact address. It takes about 60 seconds.

Is Garland a good place to buy a home in 2026?

Garland is one of the best values in the DFW metroplex right now. You get more space and more home for your money compared to Richardson, Plano, or Dallas proper, and the commute is easy via I-30 and the George Bush Turnpike. The city has been investing in revitalization, Lake Ray Hubbard adds a lifestyle bonus, and Garland ISD has solid schools. It's a smart buy for families and first-time homeowners.

What are the best neighborhoods in Garland for families?

The Firewheel area in north Garland is the most popular for families because of the newer homes, good schools, and proximity to Firewheel Town Center. Duck Creek is another great pick with its established feel, mature trees, and trail access. For families on a tighter budget, Eastern Hills offers affordable homes with good access to parks and schools. Our agents can help you figure out which neighborhood fits your family best.

How close is Garland to Dallas and other DFW cities?

Garland is right next to Dallas, about 15-20 minutes from downtown via I-30. Richardson is immediately to the west, and Plano and Murphy are to the north. Rockwall is just across the lake. The George Bush Turnpike makes it easy to get around the entire metroplex. Many Garland residents work in Dallas, Richardson, or Plano and enjoy a short, manageable commute while paying less for housing.
