Why People Love Living in Cedar Hill

Add in Uptown Village for shopping and dining, and you have a community with real character.

Cedar Hill ISD serves the community well, and the city has a diverse, welcoming population that takes pride in its neighborhoods. Cedar Hill Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Cedar Hill has a variety of neighborhoods, each with its own appeal and price range. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

High Pointe

High Pointe is one of the most sought-after neighborhoods in Cedar Hill. Newer construction, well-maintained homes, and a family-friendly atmosphere make it a top pick for buyers. The subdivision sits on elevated terrain with some of the best views in the area. Homes here tend to sell above the Cedar Hill median, and inventory moves quickly when it hits the market.

Waterford Oaks

Waterford Oaks offers established homes with mature trees and larger lots. The neighborhood has a settled, quiet feel that appeals to families and long-term homeowners. It is close to shopping along Belt Line and has easy access to Highway 67. If you are selling in Waterford Oaks, buyers appreciate the combination of space, location, and community.

Lake Ridge

Lake Ridge sits near Joe Pool Lake and offers a mix of home styles at various price points. The proximity to the lake and Cedar Hill State Park makes it popular with buyers who value outdoor recreation. Some homes in the area have water views or backing to green space. It is one of the more affordable entry points into the Cedar Hill market with strong long-term value.

Belt Line Corridor

The Belt Line corridor runs through the heart of Cedar Hill and offers a range of housing options from starter homes to mid-range properties. Uptown Village is nearby for dining and shopping, and the location gives you quick access to both Highway 67 and I-20. This area appeals to first-time buyers and investors looking for solid value in a growing community.
