Why People Love Living in Rowlett

Rowlett sits on the shores of Lake Ray Hubbard, and that changes everything about daily life here. You can paddle board or kayak after work, watch the sunset over the water from Bayside, and still be connected to downtown Dallas via the DART Blue Line. It is one of the few lake cities in DFW where you get genuine waterfront living at a price that does not shut most people out. The Sapphire Bay development is bringing a resort-style marina, restaurants, and hotels to the lakefront, and it is already raising the profile of the whole city.

The median home price is around $365K, which is remarkably affordable compared to other lake communities in the metroplex. Families appreciate the dual school district coverage from both Garland ISD and Rockwall ISD. The Bayside development along the waterfront has added walkable shops and dining, and the city keeps investing in parks and trails. If you want lake life without giving up the convenience of the metro, Rowlett is hard to beat.

Rowlett Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Rowlett has distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character and price range. From lakefront luxury to established family streets, here is what our agents see on the ground.

Bayside

Bayside is Rowlett's premier waterfront development along the shores of Lake Ray Hubbard. It features walkable streets, restaurants, shops, and a mix of townhomes and single-family homes with lake views. This is where the energy is right now. If you're selling in Bayside, demand is strong. If you're buying, expect competition for the best lots and views. It is the closest thing to resort-style living in the DFW suburbs.

Dalrock Shores

Dalrock Shores is one of Rowlett's most established lakefront neighborhoods. The homes here sit close to the water, and many have private docks or lake access. Lot sizes tend to be generous, and the tree canopy gives it a mature, settled feel. Prices vary depending on water proximity, but this area consistently attracts buyers who want the lake lifestyle in an established community rather than a new development.

Waterview

Waterview is a large master-planned community in the heart of Rowlett with a resort-style pool, walking trails, and community parks. The homes are newer construction, mostly built in the 2010s, and range from starter homes to larger family houses. It is popular with families who want modern amenities, good schools, and a neighborhood that feels active and well-maintained. Great value for the square footage you get.

Miller Heights

Miller Heights is one of the more affordable areas in Rowlett, offering older homes on larger lots with a quiet, residential feel. It is a good entry point for first-time buyers who want to get into the Rowlett market without stretching their budget. The neighborhood is close to Main Street and has easy access to President George Bush Turnpike. Buyers looking for value and potential upside should take a close look here.

What school districts serve Rowlett?

Rowlett is split between Garland ISD and Rockwall ISD. Homes on the east side of the city are zoned to Rockwall ISD, which is one of the highest-rated districts in the DFW metroplex. Garland ISD serves the western portions and has several strong schools as well. Our agents can help you find a home in the specific school zone that matters most to your family.
