Fort Worth Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Fort Worth is a big city with a lot of variety. Here's a quick look at the areas our agents know best.

Cultural District / Museum District

Home to the Kimbell Art Museum, the Modern, and the Amon Carter, this area is one of the most desirable in Fort Worth. The homes here range from charming mid-century builds to newer construction. Property values have been climbing steadily because people want to live near the museums and parks. If you're selling in this area, you're in a strong position.

Sundance Square / Downtown

Downtown Fort Worth has a completely different energy than downtown Dallas. Sundance Square is walkable, clean, and full of restaurants and entertainment. Condos and townhomes are popular here, especially with young professionals and empty nesters. It is one of the few downtowns in Texas that actually feels like a neighborhood.

TCU Area / Westover Hills

The neighborhoods around TCU are some of the most established in the city. Tree-lined streets, older homes with character, and a college-town vibe that keeps things lively. Westover Hills is nearby and offers larger lots with more privacy. Prices here run above the city median, but you are getting location and quality that holds value well.

North Fort Worth / Alliance

This is the fast-growing part of Fort Worth. New construction, new schools, and a lot of families moving in for the space and affordability. The Alliance Town Center area has shopping and dining, and commutes to both Fort Worth and Dallas are manageable. If you want a newer home at a good price, this is where to look.
