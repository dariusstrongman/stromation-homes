Flower Mound is the kind of town where families put down roots and stay. DFW Airport is just 15 minutes away, which makes it a favorite for professionals who travel.

Flower Mound Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Flower Mound has a variety of neighborhoods, each with its own character and price range. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Bridlewood

Bridlewood is one of Flower Mound's most established and beloved neighborhoods. Tree-lined streets, well-maintained homes, and a strong sense of community define this area. The neighborhood has its own pool, park, and walking trails. Homes here typically range from the mid-$400Ks to the low $600Ks. It is a great pick for families who want a settled neighborhood with mature landscaping and proximity to top LISD schools.

Wellington

Wellington is a master-planned community that offers some of Flower Mound's most impressive homes. You'll find larger lots, higher-end finishes, and a country-club atmosphere with a golf course, pools, and tennis courts. Homes here tend to start in the $600Ks and go well above $1M. If you're looking for space, luxury, and community amenities all in one place, Wellington is worth a serious look.

Canyon Falls

Canyon Falls is one of the newer master-planned communities in Flower Mound and it has quickly become one of the most popular. New construction homes, a resort-style amenity center, and miles of nature trails along the preserved canyon landscape make it stand out. Homes range from the upper $400Ks to over $800K. Buyers love the modern floor plans and the fact that everything still feels brand new.

Lakeside DFW

Lakeside DFW sits on the eastern edge of Flower Mound near Grapevine Lake and offers a unique mixed-use lifestyle. You can walk to restaurants, shops, and the lakefront boardwalk. The homes and townhomes here have a more urban feel compared to the rest of Flower Mound, with prices ranging from the $400Ks to over $700K. It is ideal for buyers who want lake access and walkability without sacrificing the Flower Mound school zone.
