Southlake Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Southlake may be a smaller city, but each neighborhood has its own character, price range, and appeal. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Timarron

Timarron is one of the most recognizable names in Southlake real estate. Built around the Timarron Country Club, this community features luxury homes on generous lots with mature landscaping and resort-style amenities. Homes here typically start in the high $700Ks and go well above $1M. The golf course, pools, and tennis courts make it a favorite for families who want an active lifestyle without leaving the neighborhood. Carroll ISD zoning adds to the appeal.

Shady Oaks

Shady Oaks is old Southlake at its best. Large wooded lots, custom-built homes, and a quiet, established feel that newer developments just cannot replicate. Many properties here sit on an acre or more, giving homeowners real privacy and space. Prices tend to run from the upper $800Ks into the multi-million dollar range. If you want a true estate feel within Southlake city limits, Shady Oaks is where you look.

Southlake Meadows

Southlake Meadows offers a more accessible entry point into the Southlake market without sacrificing quality. The homes are well-maintained, the lots are respectable, and you still get the Carroll ISD address that so many families move here for. Prices in Southlake Meadows typically range from the mid $500Ks to the low $800Ks, making it a strong option for buyers who want Southlake schools and community without the seven-figure price tag.

The Enclave

The Enclave is a smaller, more exclusive community within Southlake that attracts buyers looking for newer construction and upscale finishes. Homes here feature open floor plans, high-end kitchens, and modern design elements. The neighborhood is well-located near Southlake Boulevard, giving residents easy access to Town Square, shopping, and major highways. Prices generally start around $900K and can exceed $1.5M for the largest properties.
