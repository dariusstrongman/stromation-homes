Arlington has the Entertainment District with AT&T Stadium and Globe Life Field, a major university in UT Arlington, and neighborhoods with real character that have been here for decades. Arlington Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Arlington is a big city with a lot of different neighborhoods. Here is a look at the areas our agents work in most.

Entertainment District

This is where AT&T Stadium, Globe Life Field, and Six Flags are all within a few minutes of each other. The area around the stadiums has seen a lot of new investment, including restaurants, hotels, and mixed-use developments. Homes nearby are generally affordable and tend to appreciate well because of all the ongoing development. It is a good spot if you like being close to the action.

South Arlington

South Arlington is one of the most popular areas for families. The neighborhoods here are well-established with mature trees and bigger lots. You will find a lot of homes in the $280K-$380K range that have been updated and are move-in ready. It is also closer to Mansfield, so you get a quieter suburban feel without being far from anything.

UT Arlington Area

The area around UT Arlington has been getting a lot of attention in recent years. The university keeps growing, which means more demand for housing. This part of town is great for investors and for people who want to live in a walkable, active neighborhood. Home prices are some of the most affordable in all of DFW, especially for the location you are getting.

North Arlington / Viridian

North Arlington, especially the Viridian community near Lake Arlington, offers newer homes and a master-planned feel. Viridian has trails, a lake club, and solid school access. This area is a bit pricier for Arlington but still well below what you would pay in Southlake or Colleyville, which are just a few miles north. It is a popular choice for people who want newer construction at a reasonable price.

Common Questions About Arlington Real Estate

How much is my Arlington home worth right now?

The median in Arlington is around $310K, but prices vary a lot by neighborhood. North Arlington and Viridian are on the higher end. East Arlington and areas near UTA tend to be more affordable. Why is Arlington more affordable than other DFW cities?

A few reasons. Arlington is a bigger, more established city, so there is more housing inventory. It does not have the "new and shiny" premium that places like Frisco carry. But the location is actually better for a lot of people because you are right between Dallas and Fort Worth. That central location with lower prices makes it one of the best deals in the metroplex.

Is Arlington good for first-time home buyers?

Arlington is one of the best cities in DFW for first-time buyers. You can find move-in-ready homes under $300K, which is hard to do in a lot of the metroplex.
