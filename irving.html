Irving Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Irving has a lot of variety packed into one city. Here's a quick look at the areas our agents know best.

Las Colinas

Las Colinas is the business and lifestyle center of Irving. You've got Fortune 500 headquarters, the Mustangs of Las Colinas sculpture, great restaurants along the canal, and some of the nicest townhomes and condos in the area. It's popular with young professionals and corporate relocations. Prices here are higher than the Irving average, but the walkability and convenience make it worth it.

Valley Ranch

Valley Ranch is a large master-planned community in north Irving. It has its own lakes, trails, parks, and a community feel that is hard to beat. Families love it because of the space and the neighborhood amenities. Homes here range from mid $300Ks to the $500Ks depending on the section. It is also right off the 635 and 161, so getting around DFW is straightforward.

South Irving / Heritage District

South Irving is where you'll find the most affordable homes in the city. The Heritage District has been getting a lot of attention lately with new restaurants, small businesses, and city investment. If you're a first-time buyer or looking for more house for your money, this is the area to watch. Prices here are well below the DFW average.

Irving Arts District

The Irving Arts District is a cultural pocket that a lot of people overlook. The Irving Arts Center hosts performances and events year-round, and the surrounding neighborhoods are quiet and well-established. Homes here are reasonably priced and tend to be on larger lots. It is a good fit for people who want a calmer pace without giving up proximity to the rest of DFW.
