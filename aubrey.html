Aubrey Communities Worth Knowing

Aubrey's growth is driven by master-planned communities that offer new construction, amenities, and that north Texas lifestyle. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Silverado

Silverado is the flagship community in Aubrey and one of the biggest master-planned developments in north DFW. Multiple builders, a resort-style amenity center, pools, trails, and a real neighborhood feel. Homes range from the mid $300Ks to over $600K depending on the section and builder. New phases keep opening up, so there are options for different budgets. If you want the full Aubrey experience, Silverado is the name everyone knows.

Providence Village

Providence Village is technically its own municipality but sits right in the Aubrey area and shares the same growth energy. Homes here tend to be in the $300K-$450K range, making it one of the more affordable options in the corridor. The community has its own parks, pools, and a strong homeowner association. It is a solid pick for first-time buyers and young families who want new construction without stretching the budget.

The Trails of Aubrey

The Trails is a well-established community in Aubrey with a mix of resale and newer homes. Prices generally fall in the $350K-$500K range. The neighborhood has good amenities, walking trails, and a family-oriented atmosphere. Because it is more established than some of the newer communities, you get mature landscaping and a settled feel. Resale homes here tend to move quickly because buyers like the location and price point.

Harvest

Harvest by Hillwood is one of the newer large-scale communities straddling Aubrey and Northlake. It features a farm-to-table lifestyle with community gardens, a working farm, resort pools, and an events barn. Homes range from the $400Ks to $700K+. The concept is unique in DFW and attracts buyers who want more than just a house. If you want a community with personality and programming, Harvest is worth a close look.

It depends on your community and home size. Is now a good time to buy in Aubrey?

Aubrey is still in a strong growth phase with new communities actively building. Prices are noticeably lower than Prosper, Celina, and Frisco to the south, but they have been steadily climbing as more families discover the area. Most of Aubrey is served by Aubrey ISD, which is growing rapidly and building new campuses. Some areas on the edges may fall in Denton ISD or Pilot Point ISD.
