The city is growing fast, but it still has that small-city, college-town feel that is hard to find anywhere else in the metroplex.

Denton Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Denton has a mix of established communities and newer developments, each with its own character. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Robson Ranch

Robson Ranch is a premier 55+ active adult community on the south side of Denton. It has its own golf course, fitness center, pools, and a packed social calendar. Homes here are well-maintained and range from the mid $300Ks to over $500K. If you're selling in Robson Ranch, demand is consistently strong because the community has a reputation that draws buyers from across DFW and beyond.

Lantana

Lantana straddles the Denton-Flower Mound border (76226 and 76210 zip codes) and is one of the most popular master-planned communities in the area. Great schools, community pools, trails, and a family-friendly atmosphere. Homes typically range from the $400Ks to $600K+. Buyers love it for the space and the amenities, and sellers benefit from consistently high demand in this neighborhood.

South Lakes

South Lakes is a well-established neighborhood in southeast Denton with mature trees, a community lake, and a strong neighborhood feel. It is popular with families who want to be in Denton ISD without being too far from I-35. Homes here offer solid value compared to newer developments, typically in the $300K-$450K range. It is one of those neighborhoods that people move into and stay for years.

Idiot's Hill / Oak-Hickory

This area near the UNT campus and the historic downtown square has some of Denton's most charming older homes. Craftsman bungalows, tree-lined streets, and a walkable location that puts you steps from the square's restaurants, bars, and live music venues. Prices vary widely depending on condition and lot size, but the character here is hard to match. Investors and first-time buyers both find value in this part of town.
