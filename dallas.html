Dallas Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Dallas is a city of neighborhoods. Each one has its own feel, its own price range, and its own crowd. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Uptown / Oak Lawn

Uptown is where you go if you want walkability, restaurants, and nightlife within a few blocks of your front door. Condos and townhomes dominate here, and the energy is young and professional. Oak Lawn is right next to it with a similar vibe but slightly more affordable options. If you're selling a condo in Uptown, well-priced units tend to go fast because demand stays high year-round.

Deep Ellum / East Dallas

Deep Ellum is the creative heart of Dallas. Live music, street art, breweries, and a vibe you won't find anywhere else in DFW. East Dallas neighborhoods like Lakewood and the M Streets have beautiful older homes on tree-lined streets. Prices have gone up a lot in this area over the past few years, but it still feels like a real neighborhood, not a development.

Bishop Arts / Oak Cliff

Bishop Arts has become one of the hottest areas in Dallas. Small shops, local restaurants, and a strong community feel. Oak Cliff as a whole offers some of the best value in the city, with homes priced well below the Dallas median. Buyers who got in early have seen strong appreciation, and the area is still growing. It is a great pick if you want character and value.

North Dallas / Preston Hollow

North Dallas is where you find larger lots, established neighborhoods, and some of the best private schools in the city. Preston Hollow is the high end of this area, with estate-style homes that often sell above $1M. But there are plenty of more affordable pockets in North Dallas too, especially around the 75230 and 75240 zip codes. Families and long-term buyers love this part of the city.
