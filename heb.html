HEB Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Hurst, Euless, and Bedford each have their own personality. Here's what our agents see on the ground in the most popular neighborhoods.

Meadow Park (Bedford)

Meadow Park is one of Bedford's most sought-after neighborhoods. You get established homes on generous lots with mature trees and a real neighborhood feel. It is close to North East Mall and the Boys Ranch park area, which makes it popular with families. Homes here tend to hold their value well, and updated properties move quickly when they hit the market. If you want a classic mid-cities neighborhood, Meadow Park delivers.

Lakewood Trails (Euless)

Lakewood Trails sits in a prime part of Euless with easy access to Highway 121 and DFW Airport. The neighborhood has a mix of single-family homes that appeal to both first-time buyers and growing families. You're close to Euless's diverse restaurant scene, which includes some of the best Vietnamese, Korean, and Latin American food in DFW. For buyers looking for affordability without giving up convenience, Lakewood Trails checks a lot of boxes.

Redbud (Hurst)

Redbud is a well-established Hurst neighborhood with tree-lined streets and solid mid-range homes. It is zoned for top-rated HEB ISD schools and sits close to shopping, parks, and the Hurst Recreation Center. Homes here are typically priced below the area median, which makes it a strong entry point for buyers. Sellers in Redbud benefit from consistent demand, especially from families looking for good schools at an affordable price.

Stonegate (Bedford)

Stonegate is one of Bedford's newer and more upscale neighborhoods. The homes are generally larger and more recently built compared to other parts of the HEB area. You get a quiet, suburban feel with well-maintained common areas. It is close to TEXRail stations for easy commuting and just minutes from North East Mall. Stonegate appeals to buyers who want a more modern home in an established community with strong resale potential.
