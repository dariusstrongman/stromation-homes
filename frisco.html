Why Frisco Keeps Winning

If you haven't been paying attention to Frisco, you're missing out. This city went from about 33,000 people in 2000 to over 230,000 today, making it one of the fastest-growing cities in Texas. And it is not just suburban sprawl. There is real planning behind the growth. The PGA of America moved its headquarters here. The $5 Billion Mile along the Dallas North Tollway is filling up with corporate campuses, hotels, and mixed-use developments.

The median home price is around $550K. That is higher than some nearby cities, but you are getting a lot for it. New construction is everywhere. Frisco ISD is one of the top school districts in Texas. And there are more restaurants and things to do than you can keep up with. Frisco Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Frisco is big and getting bigger. Here is a quick look at the areas where our agents do the most business.

The $5 Billion Mile / PGA District

This stretch of the Dallas North Tollway between Lebanon Road and US 380 is where the money is going. The PGA of America headquarters, luxury hotels, corporate campuses, and high-end housing are all concentrated here. If you want to be in the center of Frisco's growth, this is the spot. Homes and townhomes in this area tend to sell quickly.

Stonebriar Area

Close to Stonebriar Centre mall, this area has a good mix of established neighborhoods and newer developments. It is a popular spot for families because of the easy access to shopping, restaurants, and Frisco ISD schools. Home prices here are a bit more approachable than the PGA district, and you still get all the benefits of being in central Frisco.

West Frisco / New Construction

If you drive west past the tollway, you will see construction cranes everywhere. West Frisco is where most of the new home communities are going up. Builders like Perry Homes, Highland Homes, and Toll Brothers are all active out here. The schools are newer, the homes are modern, and prices vary a lot depending on the builder and lot size. Having an agent who knows these builders is a real advantage.

Frisco Square / Downtown

Frisco Square is the closest thing Frisco has to a downtown. It is walkable, has restaurants and shops, and is next to the Dr Pepper Ballpark. If you want a more urban feel without leaving the suburbs, this area is interesting. It is also convenient for people who commute south toward Dallas. Townhomes and condos are more common here than single-family homes.

Common Questions About Frisco Real Estate

How much is my Frisco home worth right now?

The median is around $550K, but it really depends on your neighborhood, lot size, and how updated your home is. New construction near the PGA district can go much higher. Older homes near Stonebriar might be lower. Fill out the form above for a free report based on actual recent sales near your address.

Should I buy new construction or an existing home in Frisco?

Both have pros and cons. New construction gets you modern finishes and builder warranties, but you are usually further west and the timeline is longer. Existing homes in established neighborhoods tend to have bigger trees, bigger lots, and lower prices per square foot. Our agents can walk you through the tradeoffs based on what matters most to you.

Is Frisco expensive compared to other DFW cities?

It is on the higher end, yes. The median is about $550K compared to $425K for DFW overall. But you are getting top-tier schools, a booming local economy, and a city that continues to attract investment. If your budget is tighter, our agents can help you find pockets of Frisco that are more affordable, or suggest nearby cities like Prosper or Little Elm.

What is the $5 Billion Mile?

It is a stretch of the Dallas North Tollway in Frisco that has attracted over $5 billion in development. The PGA of America headquarters, Universal's theme park project, luxury hotels, and major corporate offices are all going in along this corridor. It is basically the engine driving Frisco's growth, and it is making nearby neighborhoods very desirable for both buyers and investors.
