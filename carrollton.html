Why People Love Living in Carrollton

Carrollton is one of the most diverse cities in the DFW metroplex, and it shows in the food. The Koreatown district along Old Denton Road is packed with Korean BBQ spots, Vietnamese pho houses, Chinese bakeries, and Indian restaurants you won't find anywhere else in North Texas. Beyond the food scene, Carrollton has DART Green Line stations that connect you straight to downtown Dallas without getting on a highway. It is centrally located between Dallas, Plano, Irving, and Lewisville, which makes it a commuter's dream.

The median home price sits around $365K, which is more affordable than neighboring Plano or Frisco. You get solid mid-range homes with good square footage, and the city is served by both Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD and Lewisville ISD depending on your location. Historic Downtown Carrollton has been revitalized with local shops, restaurants, and a walkable square that gives the city a small-town feel even though it is right in the middle of everything.

Carrollton Neighborhoods Worth Knowing

Carrollton has distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character, price range, and appeal. Here's what our agents see on the ground.

Indian Creek

Indian Creek is one of the most established neighborhoods in Carrollton. You get mature trees, larger lots, and homes that range from the $300Ks to the $500Ks depending on updates and size. The neighborhood has a community pool, parks, and easy access to the George Bush Turnpike. It is a favorite for families who want a quiet, settled feel without being far from anything. Homes here tend to hold their value well.

Country Place

Country Place is another popular family neighborhood in Carrollton with well-maintained homes, a community pool, and active HOA that keeps things looking sharp. Most homes fall in the $350K to $450K range. The location is convenient to both the Bush Turnpike and I-35E, and grocery stores and restaurants are close by. It is the kind of neighborhood where people move in and stay for a long time.

Castle Hills (Lewisville ISD)

Castle Hills sits on the west side of Carrollton and falls under Lewisville ISD, which is a draw for many families. This is a newer master-planned community with homes ranging from the $400Ks to well over $600K. You get trails, parks, a community center, and a mix of home styles from townhomes to larger single-family builds. It has a more modern, planned feel compared to the older Carrollton neighborhoods.

Old Downtown Carrollton

Old Downtown has been revitalized into a walkable area with local restaurants, shops, a craft brewery, and community events throughout the year. Homes near downtown tend to be older and more affordable, often in the $250K to $350K range, which makes this area attractive for first-time buyers and investors. The DART station is right here too, so you can get to downtown Dallas without a car.

How much is my Carrollton home worth right now?

Which school districts serve Carrollton?

Carrollton is served by two school districts: Carrollton-Farmers Branch ISD and Lewisville ISD. Which one you fall under depends on where in the city you live. Castle Hills is in Lewisville ISD, while most of central and east Carrollton is in CFBISD. Our agents can help you find a home in the right school zone for your family.
